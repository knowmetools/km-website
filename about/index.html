What We Do

Know Me is dedicated to helping people with communication disabilities connect with others and make their voices heard. Our larger goal is to help clients gain independence and better health, make their own choices and build closer relationships to sustain a high quality of life.

Our initial offerings are easy-to-use apps for people with communication disabilities and their support teams. These tools are designed to help people with communication disabilities express their unique personalities and convey their wishes and needs. They also facilitate team collaboration.

Susan Hollobaugh

CEO

Susan’s roots are in Detroit, Michigan. She worked as a teacher, an HR manager in the auto and energy industries, and an independent consultant before moving to Chapel Hill in 2004. She founded Know Me in 2013, inspired by both her work with her son Alex and her work on behalf of others who have trouble communicating their full personhood. She says, “When Alex was in middle school, I realized that I had to know him better if we were to assure a good life for him. It was the beginning of a journey that led to so many discoveries. I was increasingly aware that there were lots of people struggling with the same issue and that we needed a sustainable plan for when I wasn’t around.”

Susan loves puzzles and problem solving, and for her this work is the ultimate puzzle. “This is about giving people the tools they need to understand those who aren’t easily understood,” she says. “Alex has a dimension that is hard to put into words it’s so extraordinary. People that get close to him tend to treasure the relationship. This population can give us so much, if we just know how receive it.” Susan is happy to be part of the team working on solutions for this puzzle.

Lauren Faulkenberry

Writer

Lauren is originally from Kershaw, South Carolina. Her passion for writing and storytelling has taken her all over the southeast, but she currently lives in Whittier, North Carolina. She has worked as an archaeologist, a teacher, and a park ranger, and has spent her life collecting and crafting stories. I look forward to sharing their story with the world.”
